About Us

Welcome to My Information Just Under Glass - Network (MIJUG.NET), where technology meets community, and knowledge flows freely through the digital looking glass.

Our Mission

We’re passionate about creating connections—between people, technologies, and communities. MIJUG.NET serves as a hub for sharing practical insights about modern web development, sustainable technology practices, and building stronger tech communities across southeast Michigan, Ohio, and Florida.


Our focus is simple: empowering others through shared knowledge and fostering genuine connections in an increasingly digital world.

The Story Behind the Name

From Java Users Group to Knowledge Network

MIJUG.NET began its journey in 2002 as a platform for the Michigan Java Users Group. I created this domain while working at Compuware Corporation, supporting OptimalJ—a cutting-edge tool built on Java 5. What started as a community space for Java enthusiasts has evolved into something much more meaningful.

“My Information Just Under Glass”

The name carries both whimsy and wisdom. Like the classic Get Smart television show’s “Cone of Silence,” where Maxwell Smart and the Chief attempt secure communication under glass domes—only to discover they can’t hear each other—our digital age presents similar ironies. We’re more connected than ever, yet meaningful communication requires intentional effort.

The name reflects three key principles:

  • “Under Glass”: Information shared with transparency and protection, like viewing treasured artifacts in a museum
  • “Just Under”: The thin barrier of our screens that both connects and separates us in digital communication
  • “My Information”: Personal insights and experiences, curated with care and shared with positive intent

About Kranson Enterprises

MIJUG.NET is proudly operated by Kranson Enterprises, a technology consulting firm dedicated to making complex technology accessible and practical. We believe that the best solutions come from understanding both the technical possibilities and the human needs they serve.
