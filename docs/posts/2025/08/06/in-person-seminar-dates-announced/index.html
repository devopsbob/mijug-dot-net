In-Person Seminar Dates Announced!

Published Aug 6, 2025 • Updated Aug 22, 2025 • MIJUG Team

Fall 2025 in-person seminar dates for non-technical users to give new life to older computers that no longer support Windows 11.

#seminar#linux#e-waste#windows-11#education#diy#sustainability

🚀 Introduction

Whether you’re a retiree, a hobbyist, or someone looking to save money and reduce electronic waste, this exciting series will guide you step-by-step in converting your outdated computer into a fully usable Linux-based system!

🎯 Why Attend?

These hands-on workshops are designed to help you:

  • Understand what Linux is and how it can replace older Windows operating systems
  • Follow a simple, guided process to convert your own computer to Linux
  • Learn how to keep your computer running smoothly with free, modern tools
  • Discover how repurposing an older machine is both cost-effective and environmentally friendly

📋 Workshop Details

  • Format: Interactive demonstration with time for questions and answers
  • Experience Level: No prior technical experience required (Beginner to intermediate)
  • Cost: $20 USD (covers materials and venue; stipends accepted if needed)
  • Attendance: Open to registered attendees only
  • Seats: Very limited — secure your preferred session today!

📅 Seminar Dates & Times

Each seminar will be offered in two identical sessions on the following dates:

September 15, 18, 22, and 25

  • Session 1: 12:00 PM – 2:00 PM
  • Session 2: 3:00 PM – 5:00 PM

All seminars will be held in person at:

⚠️ Why This Workshop Matters

Windows 10 support ends October 14, 2025!

Many perfectly good computers won’t qualify for Windows 11 upgrades due to hardware requirements. Instead of discarding these machines, learn how to give them new life with Linux — a free, secure, and modern operating system!

✅ Is This Workshop for You?

This workshop is perfect if you:

  • ✅ Have old PCs gathering dust and aren’t sure what sensitive data they contain
  • ✅ Want to repurpose machines for media streaming, basic gaming, or personal projects
  • ✅ Prefer backing up to USB instead of holding onto outdated systems
  • ✅ Are curious about Linux but don’t know where to start
  • ✅ Would rather save money than buy new hardware

🍎 Mac Users Welcome!

While macOS isn’t Linux, it shares UNIX roots. We’ll explore directory structures and Terminal commands, with optional tips for modern Intel and Apple Silicon Macs.

👨‍🏫 About the Presenter

With a long career in technology and education, I’ve worked with people of all ages and backgrounds. My experience includes:

  • 🎓 Teaching at Wayne State University in Detroit
  • 👥 Leading technology user groups focused on community learning
  • 💡 Helping adults—especially career changers—gain confidence using modern technology

🔮 What to Expect

Please note: These workshops are in-person only and will not be recorded or streamed online. However, this website will remain available to support your learning, offering:

  • 💡 Helpful tips and resources for using Linux
  • 📖 Easy-to-follow guides and reference tools
  • 🤝 Continued assistance on your Linux journey

🌱 Our Mission

MIJUG is committed to reducing electronic waste and helping people get more out of what they already own. By converting an unused computer into a functional device, you can:

  • 🌐 Browse the web, send emails, and stream content
  • 👴👵 Set up a safe, easy-to-use system for grandkids or guests
  • 💰 Avoid unnecessary expenses while staying connected
    Join us and take the first step toward revitalizing your old computer — and learning something new in the process! 🎉
