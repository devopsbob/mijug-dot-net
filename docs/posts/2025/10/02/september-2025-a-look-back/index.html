September 2025: A Look Back at the Sessions

Published Oct 1, 2025 • Updated Nov 21, 2025 • MIJUG Team

Reflecting on our September PC Refresh Workshop series in Troy, Michigan - lessons learned and paths forward.

#linux#documentation#guide#tutorial

Sometimes the most valuable learning experiences happen in the most unexpected ways. Our September PC Refresh Workshop series in Troy, Michigan turned out to be exactly that kind of experience.

The Setup: Ready for Action

We came prepared for anything! Our workshop featured:

  • Four diverse demonstration computers representing different ages, architectures, and Linux distributions
  • Comprehensive backup checklists (both quick reference and detailed versions) for seamless transitions
  • Professional presentation materials including sign-in sheets, display signage, vehicle magnets, and yes - a brand new stapler (every organizer’s secret weapon!)

Originally planned for 50 attendees across 8 sessions (two daily sessions on Mondays and Thursdays over two weeks), we adapted our approach after the first week to single evening sessions on both days. Sometimes flexibility is the best feature of any good plan!

What We Learned: Quality Over Quantity

While our attendance was more… intimate than anticipated, the conversations we had were incredibly valuable. Here’s what emerged from our discussions:

The Real Pain Points

Cloud Storage Confusion: Most participants were dealing with the modern challenge of managing multiple cloud accounts, often with duplicate files scattered across different services. The “set it and forget it” approach had led to storage mysteries that needed solving.

Connection Conundrum: The hardware generation gap is real! Many folks discovered their older computers and monitors lacked HDMI ports, meaning any upgrade would require coordinating both computer AND monitor purchases.

Speed vs. Storage: We had great discussions about USB versions and transfer speeds - practical knowledge that helps set realistic expectations for backup timeframes.

Our Practical Recommendations

Based on the workshop discussions, here’s our streamlined approach to PC transitions:

1. Hardware Strategy

  • Consider your TV as a monitor - many modern TVs have HDMI inputs
  • Wireless peripherals (keyboard/mouse) are affordable and eliminate cable clutter
  • Right-size your storage - terabytes aren’t always necessary
  • External drives make excellent backup and expanded storage solutions

2. Digital Organization

  • Account inventory: Create a master list of online accounts (password manager, protected spreadsheet, or good old-fashioned address book)
  • Essential software audit: Identify which programs you actually use versus which ones just… exist
  • Cloud consolidation: Decide which cloud services you want to keep paying for

3. The Backup Process

We developed a systematic approach that prioritizes safety:

1. Create organized backup folders:
   - 'Private' (you know what goes here!)
   - 'YYYYMMDD-Backup' (e.g., '20251014Backup')

2. Separate content by type:
   - Documents, Pictures, Videos, Music on different USB drives
   - Consider compression ("zip") for faster copying

3. Safe file operations:
   - Use 'Copy' instead of 'Cut' when possible
   - Test your backups before cleaning the old system

4. Migration Timeline

Plan for patience - this process typically takes 2-4 hours and may require multiple sessions depending on file volume and cloud synchronization needs.

Looking Forward: Community Connections

The workshop materials are ready to travel! We’re happy to bring this knowledge to community groups, meetups, or organizations. Whether you prefer:

  • Group presentations for community organizations
  • Individual coaching sessions (2-4 hours, can be split across multiple meetings)
  • Phone consultations (15-minute scheduling available with 24-hour notice)

The Silver Lining

Sometimes intimate workshops provide the best learning environment. Without the pressure of a large audience, we had deeper conversations, more personalized solutions, and the flexibility to explore specific challenges in detail.

Plus, we now have a really good stapler for future events!
